Uranus
"The Planet With the Funny Name"

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and has the 3rd largest radius and the fourth largest mass of the planets (tell that to your friends). Uranus and Neptune are both in a unique category in that scientists classify them as "ice giants" instead of the "gas giants" the precede the. This is due to their compositions being much different from Jupiter and Saturn.

  1. Atmosphere
    • 83% hydrogen
    • 15% helium
    • 2.3% methane
    • < 1% Other Gases
    • Ammonia, water, ammonium hydrosulfide, and methane hydrate in ice state.
  2. Mantle
  3. Core

Uranus is also unique in that its axis tilt is unlike any other planet in the Solar System, with an axil tilt of 97.77% (as compared to Earth's 23.44% tilt). It is for this that the rings appear to be vertical instead of horizontal. Below are some other facts about Uranus (ha ha).
