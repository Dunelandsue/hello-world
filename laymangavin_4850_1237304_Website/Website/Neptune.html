Neptune
"The Blue Giant"

Neptune is the farthest known planet except for Pluto from the Sun, and the smallest of the outer gaseous planets by diameter. It gets its name from the Roman god of the sea, mainly due to its blueish hue. Along with Uranus, it is considered an "ice giant" instead of a "gas giant" because of its chemical composition, as shown below.

  1. Atmosphere
    • 80% hydrogen
    • 19% helium
    • 1.5% methane
    • < 1% Other Gases
    • Ammonia, water, ammonium hydrosulfide, and possible methane in ice state.
  2. Mantle
  3. Core

Another interesting fact about Neptune is that it has a "Great Dark Spot", which is comparable to Jupiter's "Great Red Spot", and they both have similar weather patterns. Below is some other interesting facts.
