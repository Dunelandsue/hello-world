Mercury
"The Small and Innermost Planet"

Mercury is the smallest (excluding Pluto) and innermost planet within the Solar System. It is named after the Roman messenger of the gods of the same name. Although it is fairly close to the Earth, its apparent distance never exceeds 28°, meaning it can only be seen right after sunset or right before sunrise, appearing similar to a bright star. Mercury doesn't have nearly as much of a complex structure as the Sun, as its layers consist of the following:

  1. "Atmosphere"
    • "Rock vapors" due to extreme heat.
    • No gases are present within this atmosphere
  2. Crust
  3. Mantle
  4. Core

Mercury has the shortest of all orbits around the Sun, and its rotation is unique in that it is locked in a 3:2 "spin-orbit resonance"; it rotates on its axis exactly 3 times for every 2 revolutions it makes around the Sun. For more info, see below.
