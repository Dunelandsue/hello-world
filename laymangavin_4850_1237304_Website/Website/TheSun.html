The Sun
"The Giver of Life"

The Sun is the center of the solar system and is the most important giver of energy for all of Earth. The Sun accounts for 99.86% of the total mass of the solar system. This mass mainly consists of hydrogen with the rest being mostly helium. The Sun has many different intricate layers and sublayers to itself, conprised of the following:

  1. Atmosphere
    • Chromosphere
    • Solar Transition Region
    • Corona
    • Heliosphere
  2. Photosphere
  3. Convective Zone
  4. Tacholine
  5. Radiative Zone
  6. Core

Because the Sun is the center of the solar system, it doesn't revolve around another body like the planets do, excluding the center of the Milky Way. Below is some basic facts regarding the Sun.
