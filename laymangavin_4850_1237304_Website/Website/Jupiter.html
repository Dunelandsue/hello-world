Jupiter
"The Gas Giant"

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System, but even it can fit into the Sun around 1,000 times over. It is named after the Roman god of the sky and thunder, and is about the 3rd brightest natural object in the Earthen night sky. Although it lacks a well-defined solid surface, Jupiter still has some interesting layers to itself.

  1. Atmosphere
    • 89% hydrogen
    • 10% helium
    • < 1% Other Gases
    • Ammonia, water, and ammonium hydrosulfide in ice state.
  2. Liquid Hydrogen
  3. Possible Core

Jupiter is also so massive that it has dragged a known 79 satellites (aka. moons) into its orbit, the largest of which, named Ganymede, is even larger than Mercury. For more interesting facts, please refer to the table.
