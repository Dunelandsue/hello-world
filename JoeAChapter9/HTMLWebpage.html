Beethoven's Piano Sonatas No. 1 in F Minor, No. 2 in A Major, & No. 3 in C Major

Op. 2, Nos. 1-3

The famous composer Ludwig van Beethoven completed his first three significant sonatas for solo piano during 1795-96. All three sonatas were dedicated to Beethoven's incredibly influential (and almost as famous) contemporary and tutor, Franz Joseph Haydn.

The sonatas and their movements are as follows:

  • Sonata No. 1 in F Minor

    1. Allegro
      - F Minor
      - 2/2 time
    2. Adagio
      - F Major
      - 3/4 time
    3. Menuetto - Allegretto
      - F Minor (Minuet), F Major (Trio)
      - 3/4 time
    4. Prestissimo
      - F Minor
      - 2/2 time

  • Sonata No. 2 in A Major

    1. Allegro vivace
      - A Major
      - 2/4 time
    2. Largo appassionato
      - D Major
      - 3/4 time
    3. Scherzo - Allegretto
      - A Major (Scherzo), A Minor (Trio)
      - 2/4 time
    4. Rondo - Grazioso
      - A Major
      - 4/4 time

  • Sonata No. 3 in C Major

    1. Allegro con brio
      - C Major
      - 4/4 time
    2. Adagio
      - E Major
      - 2/4 time
    3. Scherzo - Allegro
      - C Major (Scherzo), A Minor (Trio)
      - 3/4 time
    4. Allegro assai
      - C Major
      - 6/8 time
